THE MOST WELL KNOWN VENUES OF IPL ARE

M. Chinnaswamy Stadium
Bangalore

this five-decade-old stadium is situated in the heart of the city of Bangalore. It has a seating capacity of 40,000. The stadium is the home ground of the Karnataka state cricket team and the Indian Premier League franchise Royal Challengers Bangalore.

Wankhede Stadium
Mumbai

An international cricket stadium in Mumbai, India. The stadium now has a capacity of 33,108, following renovations for the 2011 Cricket World Cup. Before the upgrade, the capacity was approximately 45,000.

Eden Gardens
Kolkata

Eden Gardens is a cricket ground in Kolkata, India. Established in 1864, it is the oldest and second-largest cricket stadium in India The stadium currently has a capacity of 80,000.

Punjab cricket association
Mohali

It is popularly referred to as the Mohali Stadium. The stadium was built by Geetanshu Kalra from Ambala City and is home to the Punjab team.The stadium has an official capacity of 26,950

Arun Jaitley Stadium
New Delhi

Established in 1883 as the Feroz Shah Kotla Stadium, being near the Kotla fort, it is the second oldest international cricket stadium still functional in India. Since 2008 the stadium has been the home venue of the Delhi Capitals of the Indian Premier League.

Himachal Pradesh Cricket Association Stadium
Dharamshala

The picturesque venue is unique in India as it is situated at an altitude of 1,457 m above the sea level and has the snow-capped Himalayan mountains in the background. The stadium also hosted some IPL matches as a home stadium for Kings XI Punjab
